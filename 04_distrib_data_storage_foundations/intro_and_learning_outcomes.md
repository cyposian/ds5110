## INTRODUCTION

In this module, we will cover foundations of distributed data storage. Once we distribute data across machines, it brings challenges, and you will study practices including replication and partitioning. Object storage and filesystem storage are discussed and compared, and real-life examples are given. You will learn how to get started in AWS with Amazon S3 and EC2. Growing systems need to scale, and an effective method called consistent hashing is outlined. Finally, systems change over time, and their schema needs to change with it. We study schema evolution, challenges, and best practices.

## Learning Objectives

At the conclusion of this module, you should be able to:

- Discuss and compare object storage and filesystem storage
- Create an Amazon S3 bucket for storage and launch Amazon EC2 for compute
- Explain the benefits of data replication
- Understand the advantages and disadvantages of leader-based replication
- Explain how leader-based replication works
- Identify the purpose of data partitioning
- Discuss the limitations of mod N hashing and the benefits of consistent hashing
- Understand best practices and challenges of schema evolution
- Progress toward executing an end-to-end predictive modeling project using a large dataset
